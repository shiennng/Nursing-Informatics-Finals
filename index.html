Why NEONET

Built to solve the Philippines' most pressing maternal care gaps

Healthcare in the Philippines faces deep-rooted challenges that disproportionately affect mothers and newborns in underserved communities. NEONET is designed to directly address these.

1

Lack of services and health education in remote and rural areas

2

Insufficient resources in hospitals and health facilities

3

Facilities operating beyond capacity

4

Shortage of medical and nursing staff

5

Heavy reliance on outdated paper-based systems

"The future of healthcare is multidisciplinary. Fighting an infodemic requires communication and cooperation of professionals in various disciplines."— Dr. Isip-Tan, Philippine Council for Health Research and Development, 2023
"Telemedicine is acceptable in providing prenatal and postnatal care as it allowed the necessary interaction between patients and the medical team."— Quiñones and Virgilio, 2022

Critical factors for digital healthcare success

Internet · Innovation · Inclusion

— Dr. Batapa-Sigue, 2023

Platform Sections

Seven integrated tools for complete maternal care

Each module of NEONET is designed around real clinical workflows, ensuring information flows seamlessly between families and their healthcare providers.

01

MaternaLog

A comprehensive digital pregnancy journal capturing demographic data, obstetric history, biometrics, vitals, clinical orders, lab results, prenatal records, and the partograph — visible only to authorized health providers.

Patient DataFDAR NotesMAR
02

NeoNotes

A digital "baby book" tracking birth profile, APGAR scores, immunization records per the Philippine Vaccine Schedule, growth charts (WHO standards), developmental milestones, and medical consultations.

ImmunizationGrowth ChartsMilestones
03

EmpowerEd

A curated health education library with asynchronous videos and articles — covering pregnancy trimesters, labor, nutrition, mental wellness, warning signs, and DOH programs like Unang Yakap and the First 1,000 Days.

Lecture VideosDOH Programs
04

PharmaSync

An integrated online pharmacy module where prescriptions are securely stored alongside complete drug information — eliminating the need for in-person pharmacy visits for routine medications.

PrescriptionsDrug Info
05

NutriBase

Personalized meal plans for pre- and post-natal mothers, essential nutrients guide, foods to avoid, breastfeeding frequency logs, vitamin supplementation tracking, and a solid food introduction timeline for infants.

Meal PlansBF Log
06

CareLine

A secure communication portal enabling real-time text and chat with nurses and doctors, plus scheduled video call services — so concerns are addressed without requiring travel to the hospital.

Secure ChatVideo Calls
07

VitalSync Kit

Integrated medical devices provided to families that sync directly with the NEONET system — enabling real-time, accurate tracking and remote monitoring of essential vital signs so healthcare providers can assess patients without physical visits.

Real-time SyncBlood PressureFetal Heart RateRemote Assessment

Breastfeeding Tip

Feed on demand — at least 8–12 times in 24 hours for newborns. Ensure a good latch to prevent nipple soreness.

Folic Acid — 400–600 mcg/day

Prevents neural tube defects. Found in dark leafy greens, legumes, and fortified foods.

Iron — 27 mg/day

Prevents anemia. Found in red meat, fish, spinach, and fortified cereals. Take with Vitamin C.

Calcium — 1,000 mg/day

Builds baby's bones and teeth. Found in dairy, tofu, sardines, and dark leafy greens.

DHA / Omega-3 — 200–300 mg/day

Critical for fetal brain and eye development. Found in tilapia and galunggong.
